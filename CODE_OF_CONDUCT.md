# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

This project should be a useful, respectful place for operators, contributors,
and maintainers. People are welcome regardless of background, identity,
experience level, employer, or location.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and patient.
- Assume good intent, while still being precise about technical risk.
- Keep criticism focused on the work, not the person.
- Make issues and pull requests easier to review by including context,
  reproduction steps, and validation notes.
- Respect operational sensitivity. Do not pressure people to disclose private
  network details, credentials, logs, or security findings in public.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing someone else's private information without permission.
- Repeatedly derailing issues or pull requests after maintainers have asked for
  focus.
- Publicly disclosing vulnerabilities or exploit details before maintainers have
  had a fair chance to respond.
- Spam, impersonation, or bad-faith participation.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or delete comments; close issues or pull requests;
block users; or take other moderation steps needed to keep the project healthy.

When possible, maintainers will explain moderation decisions privately or in the
relevant thread. Serious or repeated violations may lead to permanent removal
from project spaces.

If you need to report a conduct concern, contact the repository maintainer
through the private contact options available on their GitHub profile. If no
private route is available, open a minimal public issue asking for a private
maintainer contact path and do not include sensitive details.
